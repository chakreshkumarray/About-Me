Question:
A cistern has two pipes. Pipe A can fill it in 15 min and Pipe B can empty it in 20 min. If both are opened alternately for 1 min each, starting with A, how long will it take to fill the cistern?

**Answer:** 113 minutes.

**Step-by-Step:**
1.  **Efficiencies:** Assume total capacity is the LCM of 15 and 20 = **60 units**.
    *   Pipe A efficiency = $+60 / 15 = 4$ units/min.
    *   Pipe B efficiency = $-60 / 20 = 3$ units/min.
2.  **Cycle:** In a 2-minute cycle (A then B), the net fill is $4 - 3 = \mathbf{1}$ **unit**.
3.  **Critical Point:** To avoid over-calculating, subtract Pipe A's final jump from the total: $60 - 4 = \mathbf{56}$ **units**.
4.  **Time for 56 units:** Since 1 unit takes 2 minutes, 56 units take $56 \times 2 = \mathbf{112}$ **minutes**.
5.  **Final Step:** At the 113th minute, it is Pipe A's turn. It adds 4 units: $56 + 4 = 60$ units. The cistern is now full.

### **Question 10: Permutations & Divisibility**
**Question:** How many 4-digit numbers can be formed using digits {0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5} such that the number is divisible by 3 and repetition is not allowed?

**Answer:** 96.

**Step-by-Step:**
1.  **Sum Rule:** A number is divisible by 3 if the sum of its digits is a multiple of 3.
2.  **Valid Sets of 4 Digits:**
    *   {1, 2, 4, 5} (Sum = 12)
    *   {0, 3, 4, 5} (Sum = 12)
    *   {0, 2, 3, 4} (Sum = 9)
    *   {0, 1, 3, 5} (Sum = 9)
    *   {0, 1, 2, 3} (Sum = 6)
3.  **Permutations:**
    *   **Set without 0:** {1, 2, 4, 5} can form $4! = \mathbf{24}$ numbers.
    *   **Sets with 0 (4 sets):** For each set, the first digit cannot be 0 (3 options), and the remaining digits can be arranged in $3!$ ways. Total per set = $3 \times 6 = 18$.
    *   $18 \times 4 = \mathbf{72}$ numbers.
4.  **Total:** $24 + 72 = \mathbf{96}$.

### **Question 11: Compound Interest Rate**
**Question:** A sum of money at compound interest amounts to Rs. 5290 in 2 years and to Rs. 6083.50 in 3 years. Find the rate of interest.

**Answer:** 15%.

**Step-by-Step:**
1.  **Interest in Year 3:** Subtract the Amount of Year 2 from Year 3: $6083.50 - 5290 = \mathbf{793.50}$.
2.  **Logic:** In compound interest, the amount at the end of Year 2 acts as the principal for Year 3.
3.  **Formula:** $\text{Rate} = \left( \frac{\text{Interest}}{\text{Principal for that year}} \right) \times 100$.
4.  **Calculation:** $\left( \frac{793.50}{5290} \right) \times 100 = \mathbf{15\%}$.

### **Question 12: Leap Year Probability**
**Question:** Find the probability that a leap year has 53 Sundays and 53 Mondays.

**Answer:** 1/7.

**Step-by-Step:**
1.  **Days in Leap Year:** 366 days = 52 weeks + **2 extra days**.
2.  **Possible Extra Day Pairs:** (Sun, Mon), (Mon, Tue), (Tue, Wed), (Wed, Thu), (Thu, Fri), (Fri, Sat), (Sat, Sun). There are **7** total possibilities.
3.  **Favorable Outcome:** Only the pair **(Sun, Mon)** results in both 53 Sundays and 53 Mondays.
4.  **Probability:** $1 / 7$.

### **Question 13: Boats and Streams**
**Question:** A man can row 6 km/h in still water. If the speed of the current is 2 km/h, it takes him 3 hours to row to a place and come back. How far is the place?

**Answer:** 8 km.

**Step-by-Step:**
1.  **Speeds:**
    *   Downstream speed = $6 + 2 = \mathbf{8 \text{ km/h}}$.
    *   Upstream speed = $6 - 2 = \mathbf{4 \text{ km/h}}$.
2.  **Time Equation:** Let distance be $d$. Total time is 3 hours.
    *   $\frac{d}{8} + \frac{d}{4} = 3$
3.  **Solve for d:**
    *   $\frac{d + 2d}{8} = 3 \implies \frac{3d}{8} = 3$
    *   $3d = 24 \implies d = \mathbf{8 \text{ km}}$.

### **Question 14: Algebraic Identity**
**Question:** If $x + \frac{1}{x} = 3$, find $x^5 + \frac{1}{x^5}$.

**Answer:** 123.

**Step-by-Step:**
1.  **Find $x^2 + \frac{1}{x^2}$:** $(x + \frac{1}{x})^2 - 2 = 3^2 - 2 = \mathbf{7}$.
2.  **Find $x^3 + \frac{1}{x^3}$:** $(x + \frac{1}{x})^3 - 3(x + \frac{1}{x}) = 3^3 - 3(3) = 27 - 9 = \mathbf{18}$.
3.  **Combine:** $(x^2 + \frac{1}{x^2})(x^3 + \frac{1}{x^3}) = 7 \times 18 = \mathbf{126}$.
4.  **Isolate $x^5$:** Expanding the product gives $(x^5 + \frac{1}{x^5}) + (x + \frac{1}{x})$.
5.  **Final Result:** $126 - 3 = \mathbf{123}$.

### **Question 15: Clock Angles**
**Question:** At what time between 4 and 5 o'clock will the hands of a watch be at right angle?

**Answer:** Both $5 \frac{5}{11}$ min past 4 and $38 \frac{2}{11}$ min past 4.

**Step-by-Step:**
1.  **Initial Angle:** At 4:00, the hour hand is at $4 \times 30^\circ = \mathbf{120^\circ}$.
2.  **Right Angle Condition:** The angle between hands must be $90^\circ$.
3.  **Case 1 (Before meeting):** Minute hand needs to cover $120^\circ - 90^\circ = 30^\circ$.
    *   $\text{Time} = \frac{30}{5.5} = \frac{60}{11} = \mathbf{5 \frac{5}{11} \text{ min}}$.
4.  **Case 2 (After meeting):** Minute hand needs to cover $120^\circ + 90^\circ = 210^\circ$.
    *   $\text{Time} = \frac{210}{5.5} = \frac{420}{11} = \mathbf{38 \frac{2}{11} \text{ min}}$.
