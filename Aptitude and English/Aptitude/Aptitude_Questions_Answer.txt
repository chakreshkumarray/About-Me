Q1. If x : y = 3 : 4 find the value of 3x + 4y : 5x + 6y

  Given: 𝑥 : 𝑦 = 3 : 4

Let: 𝑥 = 3𝑘 ,  y = 4k, 

Now substitute:

3x + 4y
3(3k) + 4(4k)
9k + 16k = 25k

5x  +  6y
5(3k) + 6(4k)
15k + 24k = 39k

So the ratio:

(3x + 4y) : (5x + 6y) => 25k : 39k = 25 : 39

✅ Final Answer: 25 : 39

Q2. The difference between ci and si on a sum for 3 years at 10 percent percent annum in INR 93 . The sum is?

Difference between CI and SI for 3 years = 93
Rate = 10% per annum

Formula:
Difference = P × (R/100)^2 × (3 + R/100)

Substitute values:
93 = P × (10/100)^2 × (3 + 10/100)

93 = P × (0.1)^2 × (3 + 0.1)

93 = P × 0.01 × 3.1

93 = P × 0.031

P = 93 / 0.031

P = 3000

Final Answer: 3000

Question 3.
A cistern has two pipes. Pipe A can fill it in 15 min and Pipe B can empty it in 20 min. 
If both are opened alternately for 1 min each, starting with A, how long will it take to fill the cistern?


Assume total capacity is the LCM of 15 and 20 = 60 units

    Pipe A efficiency = 60 / 15 = 4 units/min.
    Pipe B efficiency = 60 / 20 = 3 units/min.

    Cycle: In a 2-minute cycle (A then B), the net fill is 4 - 3 = 1

    Critical Point: To avoid over-calculating, 
    subtract Pipe A's final jump from the total: 60 - 4 = 56.

    Time for 56 units: Since 1 unit takes 2 minutes, 56 units take 56 * 2 = 112

    At the 113th minute, 

    it is Pipe A's turn. It adds 4 units: 56 + 4 = 60 units. The cistern is now full.

Question 4.
How many 4-digit numbers can be formed using digits {0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5} such that the number is divisible by 3 and repetition is not allowed?

Sum Rule: A number is divisible by 3 if the sum of its digits is a multiple of 3.

Valid Sets of 4 Digits:
    {1, 2, 4, 5} (Sum = 12)
    {0, 3, 4, 5} (Sum = 12)
    {0, 2, 3, 4} (Sum = 9)
    {0, 1, 3, 5} (Sum = 9)
    {0, 1, 2, 3} (Sum = 6)

    Set without 0: {1, 2, 4, 5} can form 4 * 3 * 2 * 1 = 24

    Sets with 0 (4 sets): For each set, the first digit cannot be 0 (3 options), and the remaining digits can be arranged in 3 * 2 * 1 = 6 ways. 

    Total per set = 3 * 6 = 18

    18 * 4 = 72

    Total: 24 + 72 = 96

Question 5.
A sum of money at compound interest amounts to Rs. 5290 in 2 years and to Rs. 6083.50 in 3 years. Find the rate of interest.

Interest in Year 3 -> Subtract the Amount of Year 2 from Year 3 -> 6083.50 - 5290 = 793.50

In compound interest, the amount at the end of Year 2 acts as the principal for Year 3.

Formula: Rate = Interest / Principle *  100

Rate of Interest = 793.50 / 5290 * 100 = 15 %

Question 6.
Find the probability that a leap year has 53 Sundays and 53 Mondays.

Days in Leap Year: 366 days = 52 weeks + 2 extra days

Possible Extra Day Pairs: (Sun, Mon), (Mon, Tue), (Tue, Wed), (Wed, Thu), (Thu, Fri), (Fri, Sat), (Sat, Sun)

There are 7 total possibilities

Favorable Outcome: Only the pair (Sun, Mon) results in both 53 Sundays and 53 Mondays

Probability: 1 / 7
